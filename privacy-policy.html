This Privacy Policy ("Policy") explains how ValentineOS ("we", "us", "our", or the "Developer") collects, uses, stores, and shares information when you use the Atom Translate application ("the App") on Android, iOS, macOS, and Windows. By downloading, accessing, or using the App, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy. If you do not agree with it, please do not use the App.

Atom Translate is offline-first. The App works without an account, storing your data locally on your device. Cloud features (account, synchronization, and the community) and automatic translation are optional and apply only if you choose to enable them.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information you provide

  • Account information (only if you sign up): your email address and password, or a Google account identifier if you sign in with Google (OAuth).
  • Profile information: your display name and an optional profile picture (avatar) you upload.
  • Learning content you create: folders, sections, words (original and translation), mastery levels, review dates, and optional illustration images you add from your device gallery.
  • Community content: folders or sections you choose to publish publicly, together with the categories, difficulty levels, and tags you assign.
  • Text and voice input: text you enter for translation, and audio you dictate when using the speech-to-text feature.

2.2 Information collected automatically

  • Learning activity and progress: study sessions, streaks, daily-goal progress, activity-calendar data, and per-folder statistics.
  • Community activity: decks you download or clone, and download/clone counts for decks you publish.
  • Technical data strictly necessary to operate authentication and cloud sync (for example, device and session identifiers).

2.3 Information we do not collect

  • We do not sell or rent your personal data.
  • We do not display third-party advertising and do not use advertising trackers.
  • We do not collect precise geolocation, your contacts, or health data.

3. How We Use Your Information

  • To provide core functionality: storing your vocabulary and running the study modes (flashcards, quiz, writing, matching).
  • To synchronize your data (folders, sections, words, progress, and word images) across your devices.
  • To authenticate you and secure your account.
  • To operate the community: publishing your public decks, showing your author profile, and enabling others to download or clone your shared content.
  • To translate text and convert speech to and from text when you use those features.
  • To respond to support requests and to maintain and improve the App.

4. Translation, Speech, and Third-Party Processing

Important. When you use automatic translation, the text you enter is transmitted to an external translation service for processing. When you use text-to-speech or speech-to-text, the audio or text is processed by your device's operating-system services. Please do not enter passwords, financial details, or other sensitive personal information into the translation feature.

4.1 Translation providers

Atom Translate lets you choose a translation provider in Settings. Depending on your selection, text is processed as follows:

ProviderHow your text is processed
Public third-party API (currently the default) The App currently uses the MyMemory translation API, operated by Translated S.r.l. (Italy). The text you translate is sent to their servers and processed under their own terms and privacy practices, over which we have no control. This provider is used during the App's current phase and may be replaced by self-hosted engines in future versions.
Self-hosted engines (where available) Translation runs on servers operated privately by the Developer. Text is used only to return a translation and is not used for advertising or profiling.
Manual mode No text is sent to any translation service; you type the translation yourself.

You control which provider is active and can switch to manual mode at any time to avoid sending text to any external translation service.

4.2 Speech and audio

Speech-to-text and text-to-speech rely on the speech services built into your device's operating system (for example, Apple, Google, or Microsoft). This processing is governed by your device provider's privacy policy. We do not store your voice recordings on our servers.
